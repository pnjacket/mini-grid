# Concern 11.1 — Product & Requirements

> **One-line:** What are we building, for whom, and why — and how will we know it worked. The dependency root of the entire doc set; everything downstream references it.

## Purpose & Scope

Defines the product's reason to exist and its intended outcomes, and owns the canonical **capability**, **persona**, and **success-criteria** definitions the rest of the set is traced against. It does **not** own *how* anything is built (that's Architecture/Interfaces/etc.) nor non-functional *targets* (owned by their home concerns; see Cross-references).

## Sub-aspects (the scope vocabulary)

Each may be independently in- or out-of-scope (PoC narrows; production widens):

| Sub-aspect | key | Typical default | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Problem & motivation | `problem-motivation` | always in | The "why now" |
| Target users / personas | `target-users-personas` | always in | Owned here (see Contracts) |
| Goals & success criteria | `goals-success-criteria` | always in | Measurable at Contract-grade |
| Capability register | `capability-register` | always in | The traceability spine (see Contracts) |
| Constraints & assumptions | `constraints-assumptions` | usually in | Budget, timeline, tech, regulatory constraints |
| Market / competitive context | `market-competitive-context` | often out for internal tools / PoC | |
| Stakeholders & decision-makers | `stakeholders-decision-makers` | optional | |
| Risks | `risks` | optional | May instead live in Delivery Process / Governance |

## Owned Contracts (referenced everywhere by stable ID)

1. **Capability Register** — `CAP-###`. Canonical list of product capabilities. Each entry: `id · name · description · primary persona(s) · in/out-of-scope · success-criterion ref · notes`. **Referenced by** User Experience (screens), Interfaces & Contracts (endpoints/commands), Quality & Testing (test coverage), Delivery Process (vertical slices), Accessibility & i18n. This is what lets a single capability be traced across every layer.
2. **Persona definitions** — `PERSONA-###`. Audience personas (who/why). **Referenced by** Security & Privacy (maps personas → roles/authz), Domain & Data (user entity), User Experience (journeys).
3. **Product success criteria** — `SUCCESS-###`. Observable product-level outcomes. **Referenced by** Quality & Testing and the delivery methodology's **Verified** rung. Where a capability's success *is* a non-functional target owned elsewhere (e.g. a frame-rate or latency target), its `SUCCESS-###` may be a **pointer** to that target (e.g. "meets `PERF-001`") — the criterion is still measurable, at its owner; the pointer keeps it owned once.

> **ID scheme.** `###` denotes a **stable, unique token** within the prefix's register. Numeric tokens (`CAP-001`) are the default scheme; semantic suffixes (`CAP-MODEL-CREATE`, as in the worked example below) are an **allowed variant** of the same scheme. Pick one style per register and never rename a token once it is referenced.

## Consumed (referenced, not owned)

- **Non-functional targets** — performance (Performance & Scalability), security levels (Security & Privacy), uptime (Operations). P&R links to / summarizes; it does not own them.

## Maturity Ladder — what each rung means HERE

| Rung | Meaning for Product & Requirements |
|---|---|
| **Absent** | No product definition exists. |
| **Sketch** | Problem and target audience captured as bullets; rough goals; first-pass (unstable) capability list; Non-goals noted. |
| **Specified** | Complete prose: problem & motivation articulated; personas defined; goals stated; capability register populated (named, described, persona-linked) though criteria may be qualitative; constraints & assumptions documented. A human could build with judgment. |
| **Contract-grade** | **Success criteria are measurable/observable; every capability has a stable `CAP-###` ID and an explicit in/out-of-scope mark; the register and personas are stable and referenceable by other concerns.** NFR targets are linked to their home concerns. An AI can build, and a tester can verify product-level success, without guessing. |
| **Verified** | *(delivery methodology)* The built product demonstrably meets the measurable success criteria via real-flow tests across all in-scope capabilities. |

## Contract-grade bar (the build-ready definition for this concern)

- Every in-scope capability is enumerated with a stable `CAP-###` ID and an in/out-of-scope mark.
- Every `SUCCESS-###` is phrased as an observable/measurable outcome (maps to a check). A pointer-shaped criterion ("meets `PERF-###`") satisfies this when the referenced target is measurable at its home concern.
- Every `PERSONA-###` is defined and stable.
- All referenced NFR targets resolve to a home-concern owner.

## Worked Example (a planning SaaS)

A planning SaaS's `01-system-overview.md` is this concern at roughly *Specified*. To reach *Contract-grade* it would add:
- **Personas** with IDs: `PERSONA-PLATFORM-ADMIN`, `PERSONA-TENANT-ADMIN`, `PERSONA-MODEL-ADMIN`, `PERSONA-MODEL-CONTRIBUTOR`.
- **Capability register**, e.g. `CAP-MODEL-CREATE` (Model creation), `CAP-CALC-ENGINE` (Calculations), `CAP-VIEW-GRID` (Grid view data entry), `CAP-DATA-EXPORT` (Export) — each persona-linked and scope-marked.
- **Measurable success criteria**, e.g. `SUCCESS-CONTRIB-ENTRY`: "a model-contributor can open an assigned grid view and save a valid cell edit, reflected on reload."
- NFR targets (e.g. view-open latency) **referenced** to Performance & Scalability rather than restated here.

## Design Decisions

| # | Decision | Rationale |
|---|----------|-----------|
| 1 | User definition is **split by facet**: personas here, roles in Security, user entity in Domain. | Each facet is owned once where it's reasoned about, cross-referenced by ID. Avoids a monolithic "user" doc and keeps authz mechanics out of the product narrative. |
| 2 | NFR targets are **owned by their home concerns**; P&R only references them. | Single source of truth per target ("owned once, referenced everywhere"); prevents the perf/security numbers drifting between P&R and the concern that actually reasons about them. |
| 3 | P&R owns an **ID'd Capability Register**, referenced by all downstream concerns. | The stable IDs create end-to-end traceability (capability → UX → interface → test → slice), structurally preventing the "built in one layer only" failure. |
| 4 | Contract-grade requires **measurable success criteria + ID'd, scoped capabilities**. | Aligns the concern with the core "testable acceptance" principle and makes the Verified handoff to the delivery methodology mechanical. |
| 5 | Semantic ID suffixes are an **allowed variant** of the numeric `###` scheme, not a deviation. | A validation build stalled on whether `CAP-MODEL-CREATE`-style IDs (used in this spec's own example) conform to "`CAP-###`". Either token style satisfies the scheme; what the scheme actually guarantees is stability and uniqueness, so that's what the rule names. |
| 6 | A `SUCCESS-###` may be a **pointer to a home-concern NFR target**. | NFR-shaped capabilities (e.g. "runs at 60 fps") would otherwise force either duplicating the target here (violating DD #2) or an unmeasurable criterion. The pointer is measurable at its owner and preserves owned-once. |
