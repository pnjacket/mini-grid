# Concern 11.15 — Business & Legal

> **One-line:** How the product is sold and licensed, what each tier includes, and the legal agreements and service commitments customers receive.

## Purpose & Scope

Active when the product is commercial or externally distributed — **or when a standalone legal/IP constraint exists** (e.g. a trademark clean-room rule for a non-commercial product): such a constraint activates the `ip-trademark-constraints` sub-aspect by itself, with everything commercial staying out of scope. Owns the **commercial licensing model**, **pricing/tiers**, **EULA/ToS**, **business SLAs**, and **non-commercial legal/IP constraints**. It does **not** own dependency-license *compliance* or regulatory terms (Governance & Compliance) — and the *enforcement policy* realizing a legal/IP constraint is Governance-owned (`POLICY-###`, 11.8), with this concern as the facts source.

## Owned Contracts (referenced by stable ID)

1. **License tiers** — `LICENSE-TIER-###`: maps to `CAP-###` entitlements + limits (users, models, quotas). **Referenced by** Product & Requirements (which capabilities are commercial) and the enforcement mechanism (feature flags).
2. **EULA/ToS + business SLAs.**
3. **Legal/IP constraints** — `LEGAL-###`: each non-commercial legal/IP constraint stated checkably (what must not appear / be resembled / be derived from, and on which surfaces). **Referenced by** Governance & Compliance (mints the enforcing `POLICY-###`) and Quality & Testing (the constraint's scan/check).

## Consumed (referenced, not owned)

- `CAP-###` (entitlements gate capabilities); Governance & Compliance (regulatory terms, dependency-license compliance); Performance/Observability SLOs (business SLAs reference them).

## Maturity Ladder — what each rung means HERE

| Rung | Meaning for Business & Legal |
|---|---|
| **Absent** | Internal / non-commercial product **with no standalone legal/IP constraint** (concern out of scope). |
| **Sketch** | Business model + tiers noted. |
| **Specified** | Tiers described; pricing; legal noted. |
| **Contract-grade** | Licensing model + `LICENSE-TIER-###` → capability entitlements/limits + pricing + EULA/ToS + business SLAs. An implementer can build entitlement enforcement and ship the legal surface without guessing. |
| **Verified** | *(delivery methodology)* Entitlement enforcement built; legal documents published. |

## Contract-grade bar

Licensing model + tier→capability entitlements + pricing + EULA/ToS + business SLAs + (where present) checkably stated `LEGAL-###` legal/IP constraints — each scoped sub-aspect independently, so a non-commercial product carrying only `ip-trademark-constraints` meets the bar with everything commercial in Non-goals.

## Worked Example (a planning SaaS)

`14-licensing.md` + `15-eula.md` are this concern. At *Contract-grade*:
- `LICENSE-TIER-STANDARD` / `LICENSE-TIER-PREMIUM` → entitlements over `CAP-###` plus limits (max users, max models), enforced via the `feature_flags` JSONB.
- EULA + ToS; business SLA referencing Observability's uptime `SLO-###`.
- Dependency-license compliance is **referenced** to Governance, not restated here.

## Design Decisions

| # | Decision | Rationale |
|---|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Business owns the commercial licensing model; Governance owns license compliance. | Two different meanings of "license" — revenue model vs dependency obligation — reasoned about by different people. |
| 2 | `LICENSE-TIER-###` maps to `CAP-###` entitlements. | Makes "what does this tier include" precise and enforceable, and reuses the capability spine rather than inventing a parallel feature list. |
| 3 | Owns EULA/ToS/SLA; references Governance for regulatory terms. | Customer agreements live with the commercial model; regulatory obligations stay single-sourced in Governance. |
| 4 | Contract-grade requires enforceable entitlements. | A tier that can't be enforced is marketing, not a contract; tying tiers to capabilities + flags makes them real. |
| 5 | A published `ip-trademark-constraints` key (`LEGAL-###`) covers non-commercial legal/IP constraints, and is a trigger in its own right. | All prior keys were commercial-shaped, so a real in-scope legal surface (a trademark clean-room constraint on a non-commercial validation build) was unrepresentable in the manifest vocabulary and had to ride as residual surface. The constraint facts live here; Governance (11.8) owns the enforcing policy — the legal-constraint-as-facts chain. |
